# Release Public Fixture Proof Policy

## Question

Can a controlled public fixture policy define when generated edge packages are acceptable release proof versus when binary public fixtures are required?

## Hypothesis

Yes. The policy should not make generated fixtures disappear; it should force generated proof to stay labeled, indexed with blockers, and backed by a public-corpus replacement scan.

## External sources checked

- Microsoft Protected View: https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/office/what-is-protected-view-d6f09ac7-e6b9-4495-8e43-2bbcdbcb6653
- Open Packaging Conventions overview: https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/previous-versions/windows/desktop/opc/open-packaging-conventions-overview
- in-toto Attestation Framework: https://github.com/in-toto/attestation
- SLSA attestation model: https://slsa.dev/spec/v1.1/attestation-model

## Why this matters to Ascend

Ascend's release claims need a higher bar than "the harness can generate a shape." But banning generated edge packages outright would slow package-topology proof where public fixtures are scarce. A policy lets product accept generated proof honestly without confusing it with real-world fixture coverage or signed provenance.

## Results

Generated edge packages are acceptable release proof only when the edge case is structural/package-level, generated by tracked code, labeled as synthetic/generated, carried as a release-index blocker until accepted, and accompanied by a public-corpus scan.

Public binary fixtures are required before stronger headline copy when the claim depends on real-world authoring behavior, vendor/UI/repair semantics, trust/malware/provenance language, or unresolved licensing/privacy/provenance.

Applied to safe-open: generated signed and unknown-part cases remain acceptable local proof, but they still block stronger release copy until accepted or replaced.

## Confidence

High for policy consistency with current proof artifacts. Medium for future application outside safe-open and package-action proof; each new claim should rerun the policy against its own fixture semantics.

## Fold-in decision

Promote to topic synthesis and product/correctness release stewardship. No production surfaces changed.
